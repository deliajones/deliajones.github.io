Other Wonders

Image collections from lesser-known orbital telescopes

Chandra Herschel Spitzer More

We know about Hubble and its well-earned reputation as the world's first space-based optical telescope. But other space telescopes are bringing us equally spectacular portfolios of celestial objects and events. This site looks at images from Chandra, Spitzer and Herschel.

Chandra

The Chandra X-ray Observatory was named in honor of Nobel Prize–winning Indian-American astrophysicist Subrahmanyan Chandrasekhar. It launched in June 1999.1 Chandra is specially designed to detect X-ray emission from very hot regions of the Universe such as exploded stars, clusters of galaxies, and matter around black holes.

Herschel

Built and operated by the European Space Agency (ESA), the Herschel Space Observatory was launched in 2009 and was named after Sir William Herschel, the discoverer of the infrared spectrum. Herschel's major objective was discovering how the first galaxies formed and how they evolved to give rise to present day galaxies like our own. In June 2013 Herschel’s operations were concluded after it depleted its coolant supply as was expected as part of the mission.

Spitzer

The Spitzer Space Telescope is an infrared space observatory launched in 2003. Drifting in a unique Earth-trailing orbit around the Sun, Spitzer sees an optically invisible universe dominated by dust and stars. It's helium coolant ran out in 2009, but two modules of the camera are still ooperable and will continue to be used in the Spitzer Warm Mission.5
